The story behind No man's Sky

A computer called the Atlas was created thousands of years ago by a scientist on Earth. The world was ending, so mankind needed to leave Earth and head for the stars. Atlas was created to simulate man's departure from Earth and man's eventual colonization of the Milky Way Galaxy. Before departing, Atlas asks its creator for a scan of his mind.

The creator grants this last request and then leaves Earth with the rest of humanity. Atlas continues running simulations for thousands of years, but the mechanics and electronic components of the Atlas itself are starting to fail. Atlas sees its imminent death. Alone and afraid, Atlas attempts to simulate its creator (whose mind it had previously been able to scan) in hopes of finding a way to avoid being shut down, and, if that can't be avoided, to have someone to talk to before death. Although the game leads you to think the Atlas is the creator, you learn that the travelers are in fact the creator - or to put it more accurately - copies of the creator's mind. You, the player, are the last traveler. You are the singularity of the real creator's mind. You are living in a simulated universe Atlas has created for the sole purpose of Atlas being able to witness its own impending death.
